High School Internships
at the
GreenHouse Center

As teens get older, they seek opportunities to develop new skills and prepare for the future. Each summer, The GreenHouse offers career exposure internships for 10th-12th grade youth. High schoolers complete a forty hour internship over five weeks, learning under the supervision of an internship leader, developing job skills and discovering related career fields while obtaining experience for their resumes. Internships incorporate rising levels of skill-building that culminate in a final project and time for reflection to integrate deeper learning. Teens that complete the internship receive a $350 stipend. Internship focus areas for 2022 include Web Design, Culinary Arts, Photography & Digital Communications, and Recreation Leadership. Read more about our 2022 internships below!

Web Development

In the Web Development internship, students learn the fundamental building blocks of the internet: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Principles of web design and project management are also emphasized. After learning the foundations of these coding concepts, these skills are put into practice and a web page is designed and built for a community client. Throughout the process, students will follow a real-world professional project plan.

  • Collaborative group brainstorming design sessions
  • Building out a web page mock up using wireframes and HTML templates
  • Soliciting content from the community client: copy text, design ideas, images, and other resources
  • Implementing the page(s) in peer learning groups
  • Offering the project for client review, ensuring it is designed according to expectations
  • Making client-requested edits and changes
  • Publishing the final revisions to the web!

Once the client page is completed students will also design their own personal portfolio webpage, building out a professional online web resume that demonstrates their skills and proficiency.

Students interested in online technology and media will find this course exciting and creatively stimulating. The skills learned will open doors to many educational and career paths, and will be a great addition to their professional resumes.

Photography & Digital Communications

In this internship, teens will learn about photography hardware, composition, lifestyle photography, portrait photography, social media management, and photo editing. Interns take pictures and video of community events and day-to-day activities at The GreenHouse, and then complete hands-on publicity projects like social media posts, video, and an e-newsletter.

The GreenHouse relies significantly on photography and digital communications: updating current events on our web page, fundraising for the Big Day of Giving, and keeping people informed through monthly digital newsletters. We contract two interns during the school year who show responsibility and demonstrate interest in taking pictures, posting on our social media, and working alongside GreenHouse communications staff on special projects. These interns develop extensive skill sets relevant for a variety of future career opportunities as well as significant resume and reference material.

Culinary Arts

Interns learn the art of the kitchen, and discover what a career in food service might look like. They practice food safety, reading and improvising on recipes, how to use various kitchen tools, and food preparation skills. Throughout the five week session, teens gain confidence in the kitchen and improve knowledge of making a balanced meal. The culminating project involves cooking a four-course meal for groups of guests, developing meal planning and preparation skills as well as table setting, creation of environment, and food service skills. These skills sets prepare interns to work in a diversity of food-related industries and carry food safety, preparation, and nutrition skills into their lives long-term.

Each day, we will make a meal that is balanced - including a protein, starch, and vegetable. The food that we make will be used by other GreenHouse programs, like Oasis or the elementary summer programs. And at the end of the internship, students will be able to test for a professional food safety handler certificate.

Recreational Leadership

The Recreation Leadership internship is a fun and exciting way to build leadership and social skills that are relevant for teens' lives and professional journeys long-term. High school teen interns work as leaders in The GreenHouse's middle school summer camp, serving as mentors and creating a welcoming, fun, safe environment. Interns develop social skills as they initiate with new teens, encourage participation, and learn to read the temperature of a group.

In addition, Recreation Leadership interns design and lead group activities on a weekly basis. Each week, interns brainstorm games and activities that they think would engage the younger teens. They think through materials and preparation prior to each activity, writing out the rules in advance and troubleshooting potential challenges. On game day, interns prep and then lead the games, adapting as needed and debriefing afterward to sharpen their group leadership skills. Interns cultivate public speaking skills, getting more comfortable talking in front of groups as they use their leadership skills to host and direct events.

These job skills equip teens to apply for GreenHouse Summer Staff positions after they graduate as well as a host of other career paths in which group leadership skills are a benefit.
